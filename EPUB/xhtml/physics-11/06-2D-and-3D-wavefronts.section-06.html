Chapter summary

Presentation: 23VM

  • A wavefront is an imaginary line that connects waves that are in phase.

  • Huygen's Principle states that every point of a wave front serves as a point source of spherical, secondary waves. After a time t, the new position of the wave front will be that of a surface tangent to the secondary waves.

  • Diffraction is the ability of a wave to spread out in wavefronts as the wave passes through a small aperture or around a sharp edge.

  • When a wave passes through a slit, diffraction of the wave occurs. Diffraction of the wave is when the wavefront spreads out or “bends” around corners.

  • The degree of diffraction depends on the width of the slit and the wavelength of the wave with: CLICKME\text{diffraction}\propto \frac{\lambda}{w} where CLICKME\lambda is the wavelength of the wave and CLICKMEw is the width of the slit.

For light of wavelength CLICKME\text{540}~CLICKME\text{nm}, determine which of the following slits widths results in the maximum and which results in the minimum amount of diffraction

  1. CLICKME\text{323} \times \text{10}^{-\text{9}}~CLICKME\text{m}

  2. CLICKME\text{12,47}~CLICKME\text{nm}

  3. CLICKME\text{21,1}~CLICKME\text{pm}

We know that narrower slits give more diffraction. So we need to determine which is the narrowest slit and which is the widest. CLICKME\text{21,1}~CLICKME\text{pm} is the narrowest slit (A CLICKME\text{pm} is CLICKME\text{10}^{-\text{12}}~CLICKME\text{m}). The widest slit is CLICKME\text{323} \times \text{10}^{-\text{9}}~CLICKME\text{m} (this is the same as CLICKME\text{323}~CLICKME\text{nm}).

So the slit with the maximum diffraction is CLICKME\text{21,1}~CLICKME\text{pm}.

The slit with the minimum diffraction is CLICKME\text{323}~CLICKME\text{nm}.

For light of wavelength CLICKME\text{635}~CLICKME\text{nm}, determine what the width of the slit needs to be to have the diffraction be less than the angle of diffraction in each of these cases:

Water waves at the entrance to a harbour which has a rock barrier with a CLICKME\text{3}~CLICKME\text{m} wide opening. The waves have a wavelength of CLICKME\text{16}~CLICKME\text{m} wavelength approach the opening straight on.

The angle of diffraction for the water waves is:

CLICKME\begin{align*} \sin \theta & = \dfrac{m \lambda}{w} \\ & = \dfrac{\text{3}\text{ m}}{\text{16}\text{ m}} \\ \sin \theta & = \text{0,1875} \\ \theta &= \text{10,81} \end{align*}

We need to find a slit width that will give a smaller angle of diffraction for light of CLICKME\text{635}~CLICKME\text{nm}:

CLICKME\begin{align*} \sin \theta & = \dfrac{m \lambda}{w} \\ \text{0,1875} & = \dfrac{\text{635} \times \text{10}^{-\text{9}}\text{ m}}{w} \\ w & = \frac{\text{635} \times \text{10}^{-\text{9}}\text{ m}}{\text{0,1875}} \\ &= \text{3,4} \times \text{10}^{-\text{6}}\text{ m} \end{align*}

The slit must be less than CLICKME\text{3,4} \times \text{10}^{-\text{6}}~CLICKME\text{m}.

Light with a wavelength of CLICKME\text{786} \times \text{10}^{-\text{9}}~CLICKME\text{m} strikes a single slit of width CLICKME\text{30} \times \text{10}^{-\text{7}}~CLICKME\text{m}.

The angle of diffraction is:

CLICKME\begin{align*} \sin \theta & = \dfrac{m \lambda}{w} \\ & = \dfrac{\text{786} \times \text{10}^{-\text{9}}\text{ m}}{\text{30} \times \text{10}^{-\text{7}}\text{ m}} \\ \sin \theta & = \text{0,262} \\ \theta &= \text{15,18} \end{align*}

We need to find a slit width that will give a smaller angle of diffraction for light of CLICKME\text{635}~CLICKME\text{nm}:

CLICKME\begin{align*} \sin \theta & = \dfrac{m \lambda}{w} \\ \text{0,262} & = \dfrac{\text{635} \times \text{10}^{-\text{9}}\text{ m}}{w} \\ w & = \frac{\text{635} \times \text{10}^{-\text{9}}\text{ m}}{\text{0,262}} \\ &= \text{2,4} \times \text{10}^{-\text{6}}\text{ m} \end{align*}

The slit must be less than CLICKME\text{2,4} \times \text{10}^{-\text{6}}~CLICKME\text{m}.
