Chapter summary

Presentation: 23FX

  • A vector has a magnitude and direction.

  • Vectors can be used to represent many physical quantities that have a magnitude and direction, like forces.

  • Vectors may be represented as arrows where the length of the arrow indicates the magnitude and the arrowhead indicates the direction of the vector.

  • Vectors in two dimensions can be drawn on the Cartesian plane.

  • Vectors can be added graphically using the head-to-tail method or the tail-to-tail method.

  • A closed vector diagram is a set of vectors drawn on the Cartesian using the tail-to-head method and that has a resultant with a magnitude of zero.

  • Vectors can be added algebraically using Pythagoras' theorem or using components.

  • The direction of a vector can be found using simple trigonometric calculations.

  • The components of a vector are a series of vectors that, when combined, give the original vector as their resultant.

  • Components are usually created that align with the Cartesian coordinate axes. For a vector CLICKME\vec{F} that makes an angle of CLICKME\theta with the positive CLICKMEx-axis the CLICKMEx-component is CLICKME\vec{R}_x=R\cos(\theta) and the CLICKMEy-component is CLICKME\vec{R}_y=R\sin(\theta).

Four forces act simultaneously at a point, find the resultant if the forces are:

  • CLICKME\vec{F}_{1} = CLICKME\text{2,3}~CLICKME\text{N} in the positive CLICKMEx-direction

  • CLICKME\vec{F}_{2} = CLICKME\text{4,9}~CLICKME\text{N} in the positive CLICKMEy-direction

  • CLICKME\vec{F}_{3} = CLICKME\text{4,3}~CLICKME\text{N} in the negative CLICKMEy-direction

  • CLICKME\vec{F}_{4} = CLICKME\text{3,1}~CLICKME\text{N} in the negative CLICKMEy-direction

We note that we have more than two vectors so we must first find the resultant in the CLICKMEx-direction and the resultant in the CLICKMEy-direction.

In the CLICKMEx-direction we only have one vector and so this is the resultant.

In the CLICKMEy-direction we have three vectors. We can add these algebraically to find CLICKME\vec{R}_{y}:

CLICKME\begin{align*} \vec{F}_{2} & = \text{+4,9}\text{ N} \\ \vec{F}_{3} & = -\text{4,3}\text{ N} \\ \vec{F}_{4} & = -\text{3,1}\text{ N} \end{align*}

Thus, the resultant force is:

CLICKME\begin{align*} \vec{F}_{2} + \vec{F}_{3} + \vec{F}_{4} & = (\text{4,9}) + (-\text{4,3}) + (-\text{3,1}) \\ & = -\text{2,3} \end{align*}

Now we use CLICKME\vec{R}_{x} and CLICKME\vec{R}_{y} to find the resultant.

We note that the triangle formed by CLICKME\vec{R}_{x}, CLICKME\vec{R}_{y} and the resultant vector is a right-angle triangle. We can thus use the Theorem of Pythagoras to determine the length of the resultant. Let CLICKMER represent the length of the resultant vector. Then: CLICKME\begin{align*} F_x^{2} + F_y^{2} &= R^{2}\ \textrm{Pythagoras' theorem}\\ (\text{2,3})^{2} + (-\text{2,3})^{2} &= R^{2}\\ R &= \text{3,25}\text{ N} \end{align*}

To determine the direction of the resultant force, we calculate the angle α between the resultant force vector and the positive CLICKMEx-axis, by using simple trigonometry: CLICKME\begin{align*} \tan\alpha &= \frac{\textrm{opposite side}}{\textrm{adjacent side}} \\ \tan\alpha &= \frac{\text{2,3}}{\text{2,3}} \\ \alpha &= \tan^{-1}(\text{1}) \\ \alpha &= \text{45}\text{°} \end{align*}

The resultant force is then CLICKME\text{3,25}~CLICKME\text{N} at CLICKME\text{45}CLICKME\text{°} to the CLICKMEx-axis.
