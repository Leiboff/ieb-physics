The speed of light

One of the most exciting discoveries in physics during the last century, and the cornerstone of Einstein's Theory of Relativity, is that light travels at a constant speed in a given medium. Light also has a maximum speed at which it can propagate, and nothing can move faster than the speed of light. The maximum speed at which light can travel is when it propagates through free space (a vacuum) at CLICKME\text{299\ 792\ 485}\text{ m·s^{-1}}. A vacuum is a region with no matter in it, not even air. However, the speed of light in air is very close to that in a vacuum.
