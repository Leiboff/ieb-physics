Consider three point sources of waves. If each source emits waves isotropically (i.e. the same in all directions) we will get the situation shown in as shown in Figure fig-p-wsl-2d3d12-wavefront below.

Wavefront

A wavefront is an imaginary line that connects waves that are in phase.

We define a wavefront as the imaginary line that joins waves that are in phase. These are indicated by the grey, vertical lines in Figure fig-p-wsl-2d3d12-wavefront. The points that are in phase can be peaks, troughs or anything in between, it doesn't matter which points you choose as long as they are in phase.
